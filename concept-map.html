How to Build a Concept Map

Read through all the slides first, then follow along as you make your own concept map.

1. Identify Key Concepts

Identify key concepts from the introduction and list them. Some people find it helpful to write them on separate notecards so that they can be moved around.

2. Organize the Concepts

Rank the concepts from the broadest and most inclusive to narrowest and most specific. Begin to build your map by placing the most inclusive and general concept(s) at the top. Usually, there will only be one, two, or three concepts at the top of the map.

3. Add Subconcepts

Select two, three, or four subconcepts to place under each general concept. Avoid placing more under any other concepts—if there seem to be six or eight concepts that belong under a major concept, it is usually possible to identify some intermediate concept, thus creating another level of hierarchy in your map.

4. Connect the Concepts

Connect the concepts with lines. Label the lines with one or a few linking words that define the relationship between the two concepts.

5. Rework the Concept Map

Try reworking the structure of your map, which may include reorganizing concepts, redefining relationships, and adding lines. You can do this as many times as you like—there is no one way to make a concept map. This is where notecards are helpful!
